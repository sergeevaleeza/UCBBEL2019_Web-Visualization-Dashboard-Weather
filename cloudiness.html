Humidity (%) vs. Latitude

Continents tend to be less cloudy than adjacent oceans (at the same latitude), except in the equatorial belt. On average, cloudiness is highest around 60 degrees South (about 80% of the time) but there is only 40% cloud cover at the South Pole. These figures reflect the ascent and subsidence occurring at these respective latitudes, and the large amount of ocean at around 60 degrees South. The subsidence at 20 - 30 degrees latitude in both hemispheres leads to 45 - 50% cloud cover there. This is mostly stratus clouds off the west coasts of continents, and cumulonimbus clouds over the continents in summer. At latitudes above 50 degrees North, the annual average cloud cover is about 70%.
